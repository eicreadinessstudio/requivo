Step 01 / 06

Problem
Operational knowledge is everywhere — but rarely ready to use
Most organisations do not fail because information does not exist. They fail because the knowledge they depend on is fragmented, difficult to validate, and often concentrated in a few individuals.
01

Expertise stays in people’s heads

Critical know-how often lives in individual experts, informal workarounds, and local context — instead of becoming reusable organisational knowledge.

02

Documents do not transfer judgment

Teams may inherit files and procedures, but not the reasoning, exceptions, decisions, and operational context behind them.

03

Answers remain fragmented

Relevant signals are spread across systems, versions, and teams. Without validation and ownership, it is hard to know what is current, complete, or reliable.

Solution
Requivo creates a governed operational knowledge layer from scattered enterprise knowledge

Requivo works where critical knowledge is distributed across documents, systems, workflows, and expert know-how.

It structures fragmented inputs into a governed knowledge layer, surfaces weak or conflicting areas, and routes unresolved knowledge to the right experts for validation.

The result is not just a better answer. It is a reusable operational knowledge asset that can support writing, verification, management, and controlled AI access.

Core flow

Fragmented evidence → structure → governed knowledge

Connect scattered evidence and transform it into maintained operational knowledge.

Validation loop

Gaps become expert tasks

Application layer

Why Requivo
Better retrieval is not enough when the underlying knowledge is fragmented
Requivo focuses on the layer before answers: the structure, validation, ownership, and maintenance of operational knowledge.
Fragmented
Critical knowledge is distributed across files, systems, workflows, and experts — often without a single maintained structure.
Weakly structured
Existing information rarely reflects real operational hierarchy, ownership, currentness, and reliability.
Expert-dependent
Operational judgment, exceptions, and edge-case reasoning often remain with individual experts until they are lost.
Validated
Reliable knowledge requires validation, ownership, and a process for resolving weak or conflicting areas.
Governed
The goal is a maintained knowledge layer with traceable structure, clear ownership, and controlled reuse.
Reusable
Once governed, operational knowledge becomes an organisational asset for teams, workflows, and AI systems.
Use cases
