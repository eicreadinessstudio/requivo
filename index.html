Operational knowledge system

Critical knowledge exists. Your organisation just can’t use it.

Requivo is designed to turn fragmented enterprise evidence into governed operational knowledge — structured, validated, and reusable across workflows where continuity, judgment, and trust matter.

Mission

To turn fragmented enterprise evidence into governed operational knowledge that organisations can trust, maintain, and reuse.

Vision

A world in which organisations no longer rely on scattered documents and tacit memory, but operate on governed, validated, and evolving knowledge.

Problem

Critical operational knowledge is often scattered across systems, documents, workflows, and individual experts.
Most organisations do not fail because information does not exist. They fail because the knowledge they depend on is fragmented, weakly structured, difficult to validate, and often concentrated in a few individuals. Documentation platforms, search tools, and manual transfer processes help only partially — but they do not create a maintained governed layer of operational knowledge.

Expertise disappears

Critical know-how often remains embedded in individual people, workarounds, and context that never become properly structured or reusable across the organisation.

Knowledge never fully transfers

Teams may inherit documents, but not the reasoning, hierarchy, conflict resolution, and operational judgment behind them.

Answers remain fragmented

Relevant signals are distributed across documents, enterprise systems, workflows, codebases, and expert input, making them difficult to trust, maintain, and act upon together.

Solution

Requivo is not designed as a documentation platform, an enterprise search tool, or a generic AI assistant.
It is designed as an operational knowledge system that starts with fragmented enterprise evidence and turns it into a governed operational knowledge layer that can be validated, maintained, and reused across the organisation.

Requivo works on heterogeneous source material such as enterprise systems, documents, workflows, and expert input. It does not assume that the source material is already clean, current, or well organised. Its role is to infer order from that pile.

At the centre of the concept is a proprietary knowledge-processing core designed to infer structure and hierarchy, identify gaps and conflicts, detect duplicates and drift, support validation workflows, and produce a governed operational knowledge layer in reusable form.

That governed layer is intended to support multiple downstream applications such as knowledge writing, knowledge management, expert verification, and controlled external access — which is why Requivo should be understood as a full system rather than a single interface.

Core flow

Fragmented evidence → structure → governed layer

The public concept should communicate the transition from messy source material to reusable governed operational knowledge, without exposing implementation detail.

Positioning

Beyond retrieval

Requivo is designed to go further than retrieval-oriented approaches by supporting validation, conflict and gap detection, and structured operational reuse.

Applications

One governed layer, multiple uses

The resulting layer is intended to support writing, management, verification, and controlled external AI or tool access.

Why this matters

Organisations do not only need better retrieval. They need a way to turn fragmented operational evidence into governed clarity.
Fragmented
Critical knowledge often starts as disconnected evidence across documents, systems, workflows, and experts.
Weakly structured
Even when information exists, it is rarely organised in a way that reflects real operational hierarchy, currentness, and reliability.
Expert-dependent
Undocumented judgment, tacit knowledge, and edge-case reasoning stay concentrated in individuals and are hard to preserve.
Validated
Useful operational knowledge requires explicit verification, follow-up elicitation, and the ability to surface weak or conflicting areas.
Governed
The goal is not just a better answer, but a maintained operational knowledge layer with traceable structure and reusable logic.
Reusable
Once governed, operational knowledge can support multiple workflows — from writing and management to verification and controlled external access.

Use cases

Requivo is relevant wherever critical operational know-how is fragmented, weakly documented, difficult to validate, and at risk of being lost.

Operations & manufacturing

For environments where procedures, incidents, engineering context, and maintenance know-how need to remain usable beyond individual employees and disconnected systems.

Engineering & technical services

For settings where documentation, configuration, code, and practical judgment must remain aligned and reusable across teams and over time.

Regulated and process-heavy environments

For organisations that need stronger traceability, better validation, and more maintainable operational knowledge than conventional retrieval or documentation tools can provide.
